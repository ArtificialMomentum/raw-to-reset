Day 3 — Raw to Reset

You’re not lazy — you need fuel.

Cycle Lens

  • Mid-cycle energy can feel uneven — your hormones are shifting gears.
  • Estrogen rises while progesterone begins its gentle climb — you’re recalibrating, not crashing.
  • Your nervous system and metabolism are adjusting to new demands for protein and stability.

Quick Win

  • Mid-morning anchor: add 20–30 g protein + fiber + minerals. This steadies blood sugar and brings your brain back into usable focus.

Signals to Notice

  • Motivation dips or irritability? Not laziness — it’s chemistry shifting gears.
  • “Estrogen is peaking, and progesterone is rising.” That changes how your body uses glucose and neurotransmitters — so your usual pace might suddenly feel “off.”
  • Feeling like your body’s “shoulds” are arguing with your energy? You’re not failing — you’re recalibrating.

Support Moves

  • Front-load protein + fiber — steady blood sugar = steady mood.
  • Move to spark, not drain — brisk walk or short weighted session boosts dopamine without tipping cortisol.
  • Hydrate steadily — minerals keep estrogen’s energy lift smooth instead of spiky.

→ Consistency fuels clarity. You’re teaching your brain what steady feels like again.

Reflection Prompt 🪶

What felt easier to start today? Where did focus flow without forcing it?

→ Celebrate stability — this is your brain catching its rhythm.

Why (mini-physiology)

As estrogen declines and progesterone takes the lead, your brain’s glucose use slows slightly — especially in the prefrontal cortex (focus, drive, planning). Supporting this with balanced meals and recovery movement helps prevent the “mid-cycle dip.”

Why it matters: supporting this phase with balanced meals and recovery movement keeps your nervous system steady and stops mid-cycle crashes.

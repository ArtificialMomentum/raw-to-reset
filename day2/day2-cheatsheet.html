Day 2 — Feeling Edgy? PMT Isn’t the Problem

Your luteal phase is asking for this

🧭 Cycle Lens

  • Mid-luteal (around 10–12 days pre-bleed) is when small misalignments feel amplified. You’re not “overreacting” — your brain is surfacing truth.
  • Chemistry shifts from performance to protection. When things feel off, your body’s asking for boundaries, not blame.

🌙 Quick Win

Tonight: dim the lights and slow stimulation two hours before bed — circadian chemistry loves predictability. Add a food-based B-complex with dinner (leafy greens, seeds, or nutritional yeast). It steadies progesterone metabolism and smooths your serotonin curve.

🧪 Signals to Notice

  • Snappy, teary, “done with everyone” feelings — this is data, not defects.→ Your brain is flagging overstimulation and a serotonin dip — not failure, just feedback.
  • “It’s not you, it’s your chemistry.”→ Estrogen dips and progesterone rises — shifting serotonin + GABA, your calm-and-steady messengers.
  • Saying “yes” while your face says “hell no”?→ The luteal phase thins emotional filters. Your feelings aren’t wrong — they’re just louder. Protect your peace and postpone heavy conversations.

🧰 Support Moves

  • Ground with minerals + protein — magnesium-rich foods + lean protein at dinner calm the evening drop.
  • Guard your wind-down — blue-light off 2 hours before bed; keep lights low and thoughts lighter.
  • Slow input — journal, stretch, or tidy small spaces. You’re helping the nervous system file the day, not fix it.
→ These rituals re-signal safety so hormones can recalibrate overnight.

💭 Reflection Prompt

Where did honesty show up today? Did slowing down create clarity instead of guilt?

→ Note moments your body asked for stillness — and what happened when you listened.

🧠 Why (mini-physiology)

As progesterone climbs and estrogen falls, serotonin (mood) and GABA (calm) both shift — making the brain quicker to react and slower to settle. Knowing this helps you support yourself instead of self-critiquing.
